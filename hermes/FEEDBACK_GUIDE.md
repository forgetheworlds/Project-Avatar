# How to Give Research Feedback

Your feedback teaches me what research is valuable so I can do better each night.

## Quick Feedback (Just Say)

```
"Last night's research was 4/5 — the Gazebo findings saved me time"
"Research quality 2/5 — I already knew most of that"
"Can you research [topic] tonight instead of choosing yourself?"
```

## Detailed Feedback (Use Template)

Copy `nightly/FEEDBACK_TEMPLATE.md` and fill it out.

## What Happens to Your Feedback

| Feedback Type | How I Use It |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Rating (1-5)** | Learn which topics/sources are valuable |
| **What worked** | Do more of that style/topic |
| **What was missing** | Add to tonight's research queue |
| **Corrections** | Update my understanding + correct future research |
| **Next topics** | Prioritize for tonight's 5 questions |

## Example Good Feedback

```
"Quality: 4/5

The MAVSDK offboard best practices were exactly what I needed — 
I didn't know about pre-streaming setpoints. 

Missing: I'd like to see more on testing patterns for SITL vs hardware.

For tonight: Research how others structure pytest fixtures for drone tests."
```

## Research Priority Topics

These always get high priority:
1. Things that block current work
2. Technical decisions you're about to make
3. Performance validation (is 10 FPS realistic?)
4. Competitive intelligence (who else is building this?)
5. Safety best practices

Let me know what's urgent and I'll prioritize it.
